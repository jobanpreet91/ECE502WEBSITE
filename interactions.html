Adult–Child Interactions and Home Learning Environments

Positive adult–child interactions play a key role in supporting language development. Children learn language best through warm, responsive, and meaningful communication.

  • Talking with children during daily routines
  • Reading books together
  • Singing songs and nursery rhymes
  • Asking open-ended questions
  • Listening and responding to children’s ideas

Home learning environments that are rich in language help children develop confidence, curiosity, and strong communication skills.
