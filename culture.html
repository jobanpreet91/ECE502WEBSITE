Cultural and Linguistic Responsiveness

Children grow up in families with diverse languages, cultures, and traditions. Cultural and linguistic diversity should be respected and celebrated in children’s learning environments.

Families are encouraged to continue using their home language with their children. Speaking more than one language supports children’s identity and does not delay English language development.

Songs, stories, cultural celebrations, and everyday conversations help children feel a sense of belonging and confidence. Valuing children’s culture and language supports positive communication and learning.
